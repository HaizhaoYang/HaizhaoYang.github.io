Finite Expression Method for Discovering Governing Equations from Data

Haizhao Yang, Zhiwen Zhang, and collaborators

arXiv Code PDF

Overview

The Finite Expression Method (FEX) is a symbolic-numeric approach for discovering compact mathematical expressions that describe governing equations directly from observational data. Unlike purely numerical methods that produce black-box models, FEX outputs human-readable formulas that capture the underlying physics.

The key insight is to search over a space of finite expression trees, where each tree represents a composition of elementary operations (addition, multiplication, elementary functions). By combining this structured search with neural network-based optimization, FEX achieves both the expressivity needed to fit complex data and the interpretability demanded by scientific applications.

FEX has been successfully applied to discover governing equations in dynamical systems, partial differential equations, and conservation laws, often recovering the exact symbolic form from noisy observations.

Method

Figure 1: Expression tree search space

Figure 2: Optimization landscape

The method works in two stages. First, a neural network is trained to approximate the target function from data. Second, the trained network is distilled into a compact symbolic expression through a structured search over finite expression trees. This two-stage approach avoids the combinatorial explosion of direct symbolic regression while maintaining the ability to discover exact formulas.
